Strawberry Ice Cream

Strawberry Ice Cream is one of the most common flavors of ice cream available. However, most people do not realize that strawberry ice cream is more difficult to create due to problems when balancing water content in the mixure. Most recipes do not account for the extra water content in strawberries which causes more ice crystal causing the mixture to be less smooth.

In order to counteract the extra water in the mixture, most ice cream recipes uses strawberry extract or powdered strawberries. I found that using a combination of dried strawberries and a strawberry syrup was best for keeping water to a minimum.

Ingredients

  • 2 Cups Heavy Whipping Cream
  • 1 Cups Whole Milk
  • 3 1/2 Cups Sugar
  • 1 Tpbs. Strawberry Extract
  • 1 Cup of Water
  • 1/2 Cup Dried Strawberries

Steps

  1. Simmer 1 cup of sugar and 1 cup of water until a simple syrup forms.
  2. Add the dried strawberries, heavy whipping cream,whole milk, and strawberry extract into the syrup
  3. Let the mixture cool in the fridge.
  4. Pour the ice cream base into the ice cream mixer tub and use ice cream maker as specified.
  5. Once the ice cream base has the texture of soft serve, pour the ice cream into a container and freeze overnight.
  6. Enjoy!
